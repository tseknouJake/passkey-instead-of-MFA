Authentication Methods Study
// About this study

Welcome to AuthLab, a university research platform comparing modern authentication methods. This study explores the usability, security, and user experience of four login flows commonly used across the web. Your responses will be used for academic research purposes only.

// Before you begin
📱

The MFA method requires a TOTP authenticator app on your mobile device. Please install Google/Microsoft Authenticator (free on iOS & Android) before starting.

// How to participate
  1. Create an account Register with a username and password. This single account is used across all four authentication methods. We suggest against reusing credentials that you have used previously on other platforms.
  2. Set up an authentication method of your choice After registering, complete the one-time setup for one of the methods: MFA (TOTP), Passkey, and Google login.
  3. First, log in using the old-fashioned username and password Return to the login page and authenticate using the username and password. After a successful login you will be taken to your dashboard.
  4. Complete the survey After the login, fill in the short questionnaire about that method. Answer based on that specific experience. This takes about 2–3 minutes.
  5. Log out and choose at least one more authentication method Log out, then log back in using the next method.
  6. Additionally: Complete other authentication methods It would really help us in our research if you could complete the other remaining methods by repeating the steps above
  7. Optional: Security training A security awareness simulation is available from your dashboard at any time. This is entirely optional and does not affect your survey responses.
